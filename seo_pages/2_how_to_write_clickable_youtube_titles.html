A title is a promise. If the promise is boring, nobody cares about the payoff.

Step 1: The Curiosity Gap

The curiosity gap is the space between what a viewer knows and what they want to know. If your title says "I bought an expensive car," the gap is closed. But if it says "The massive mistake I made buying my first car," the gap is wide open. They have to click to see the mistake.

Use "Power Words" like: Regret, Secret, Warning, Mistake, Finally, or Unexpected.

Step 2: Negative vs. Positive Triggers

Humans are biologically wired to avoid pain more than we are to seek gain. This is why "5 things you are doing wrong" usually performs better than "5 things you should do." We are terrified of missing out or being the person making a mistake. This is a core part of improving your YouTube CTR.

Step 3: Extreme Specificity

Vague titles die. Instead of "I built a house," use "I built a house in 24 hours with $500." The specificity makes the claim feel more real and more impressive. If you are struggling with low engagement, specificity is your best friend.

The shorter the title, the harder it hits. Aim for under 50 characters to avoid being cut off on mobile.
